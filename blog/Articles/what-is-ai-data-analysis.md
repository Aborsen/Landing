The analyst then spends hours writing custom SQL or exporting raw data into fragile, manual spreadsheets that are outdated the moment they are saved.

Even with powerful infrastructure like BigQuery, the technical floor remains too high for the average operations leader. While these platforms offer immense power, they lack the accessibility of a modern **ai analyst**. ### Why Traditional BI Fails the Business User

Traditional "self-service" BI tools often fail because they are built for builders, not for users. Once a dashboard is finally delivered, it is usually a collection of static charts that offer no room for exploration. If a user sees a dip in conversion and wants to ask "Why?", they are forced back into the ticket queue.

True **business intelligence automation** is about removing this friction entirely. Traditional BI falls short for several specific reasons:

1.  **Technical prerequisites:** Most tools marketed as "self-service" still require users to understand complex table schemas or write basic queries.
2.  **Lack of dialogue:** You cannot ask a follow-up question. If the chart doesn't show the specific breakdown you need, you cannot drill down without starting over.
3.  **Fragmented definitions:** Without a centralized system, every user builds their own version of a metric, leading to conflicting numbers across different departments.

This reliance on manual processes doesn't just slow things down—it fundamentally limits how a company can use its information to grow.
