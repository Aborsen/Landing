# What is Self-Service BI? The Guide to True Data Democratization

Self-service business intelligence (BI) is a model where business users access and analyze data independently, without waiting for a technical expert. It aims to turn every employee into a data-driven decision-maker by removing the barriers between raw data and actionable answers.

However, most companies are living with the "Self-Service Lie." Legacy tools promise easy access but still require SQL skills or complex configuration just to see a simple trend. Instead of actual independence, teams remain buried in **ad-hoc data requests** and manual spreadsheet assembly. True self-service doesn't mean building another dashboard; it means using an **AI Chat** grounded in a **Semantic Layer** to get the right answer in seconds, not weeks.

## FAQ Section

### What is the main difference between self-service BI and traditional BI?
Traditional BI is IT-led, requiring a centralized data team to build and distribute reports. In contrast, **self service bi** empowers business users to access data themselves. However, true self-service (Agentic Analytics) goes further by removing the need for SQL or manual dashboard design, allowing users to ask questions in plain language and get instant results.

### Why do most self-service BI projects fail?
Most projects fail due to the "Trust Gap" and technical complexity. When different teams define metrics like "Revenue" differently, leadership loses faith in the numbers. Additionally, if the tool still requires SQL, adoption among non-technical staff remains low, forcing them back into manual CSV exports and fragmented spreadsheets.

### How does a Semantic Layer improve self-service analytics?
A **semantic layer** acts as a "Single Source of Truth." It stores certified definitions of your KPIs in a **metrics catalog**. When an **AI Chat** interface uses a **semantic layer**, it ensures that every answer is grounded in your company's specific business logic, facilitating **hallucination prevention** and eliminating inconsistent numbers across teams.

No. Insightis is a no-code platform designed for business users. It uses advanced **text-to-SQL** technology to translate plain-language questions into database queries automatically. Users get **instant visualizations** and written explanations without ever touching a line of code or waiting for an analyst.
