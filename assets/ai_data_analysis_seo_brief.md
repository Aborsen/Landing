## AI STRATEGY & REASONING

### Scraped Competitor Analysis
The competitor landscape for "ai data analysis" is split into three distinct categories:
1.  **Tech Giants (IBM, Google, Microsoft):** These focus on high-level definitions, types of analytics (descriptive to prescriptive), and cloud infrastructure. They lack the "quick start" appeal for non-technical users.
2.  **Point Solutions (Julius, Rows, Formulabot):** These are tool-specific and focus heavily on spreadsheet integration and immediate utility.
3.  **Educational/Guides (Coursera, Luzmo, Databricks):** These provide long-form technical depth but often fail to provide a curated, categorized list of the modern tool stack.

**Gaps Identified:**
*   **The "Hybrid" Gap:** Most articles are either a dry definition (IBM) or a shallow tool list. There is a massive opportunity for a comprehensive guide that explains the "how-to" and then provides a categorized list of tools based on user persona (Enterprise vs. SMB).
*   **Human-AI Collaboration:** Very few competitors focus on the *workflow* change—specifically how a data analyst's role shifts from "querying" to "auditing."
*   **Categorized Listicles:** While some provide tool lists, they are often flat lists. A categorized approach (e.g., "AI for SQL" vs. "AI for Data Visualization") addresses specific intent better.

### Strategy to Beat the Top 10
To outrank high-authority domains, we will employ a "Volume + Categorization" strategy. We will match the technical depth of Google/IBM (word count ~4,000) while providing the immediate utility of a listicle. We will use an "Answer-First" methodology to capture Featured Snippets for "What is AI data analysis?" and "How does AI help in data analysis?"
